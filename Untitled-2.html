MTech in Techno-Entrepreneurship

Course Structure and Curriculum AY 23-25

Objectives of the Program:

To nurture entrepreneurial mindset among technology graduates and equip them with necessary skills and knowledge in entrepreneurship.

To provide an ecosystem where the students can convert their entrepreneurial ideas into successful ventures.

Scope:

Fresh and early career engineering graduates who are passionate about choosing entrepreneurship as a career would be selected for the program.

Salient Features:

  • The program is offered in the MoE (Ministry of Education) and Self-sponsored categories.
  • Candidates with BTech or a BE degree, including early career professionals with 60% Marks or equivalent CGPA, are eligible.
  • GATE or any other national level test scores are not required for the Self-sponsored category.

Objectives of the Program

To nurture entrepreneurial mind-set among technology graduates and equip them with necessary skills and knowledge in entrepreneurship. To provide an ecosystem where the students can convert their entrepreneurial ideas into successful ventures.

Scope

Fresh and early career engineering graduates who are passionate about choosing entrepreneurship as a career would be selected for the program.

Salient Features

  • The program will be a completely self-sponsored one.
  • Candidates with a BSc, B.Des, B.Arch, BTech or a BE degree including early career professionals with 60% Marks or equivalent CGPA are eligible.
  • Selection will be based on a written test and interview.
  • GATE or any other national level test scores are not required.

Course Structure

Semester 1

  • 9 credits of core EM courses + 2 credits of Management Electives + 3 credits of Engineering Electives + 1 credit Communication Skills (15 Credits)

Semester 2

  • 7 credits of core EM courses + 2 credits of Management Electives + 3 credits of Engineering Electives + 1 Credit Industrial Lectures (13 Credits)

Semester 3

  • Project Stage I - Problem Definition & Business Plan Development and Mentoring (12 Credits)

Semester 4

  • Project Stage 2 - Developing a Prototype or Proof of Concept (12 Credits)
